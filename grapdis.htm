Graphic Designer Skills: Definition and Examples

Updated February 16, 2023

Video: A Day in the Life of a Graphic Designer
In this video, we follow Jamie, a freelance graphic designer, who specializes in poster and zine design.
Graphic designers are creative professionals who use software tools to make captivating advertisements, logos, web pages or other marketing materials for their clients. Becoming a successful graphic designer may require a wide range of skills, including time management, communication and coding. Understanding what these competencies are and how to hone them can help individuals pursue a career in this field.In this article, we define what graphic designer skills are, review a list of examples, describe how to improve them and discuss how to apply them in the workplace.

What are graphic designer skills?

Graphic designer skills are creative abilities these professionals rely on to excel in their role. A graphic designer may work in-house at a design or advertising agency, in the marketing department of an organization or independently as a freelance contractor. They create print or digital content for clients that communicate brands' messages or values using visually appealing images, colors and fonts. These professionals may earn a bachelor’s degree or complete another professional training program, which can help them complete tasks such as:
  • Brainstorming design concepts and presenting these ideas to clients
  • Using design software applications to create captivating images, graphics, web pages or logos
  • Collaborating with clients to determine their goals, style guides or branding guidelines for graphic designers to reference when creating their designs
  • Choosing proper fonts, photos and colors to use on certain layouts
  • Applying clients' requested changes to designs as requested 
Successful graphic designers typically apply a mixture of hard and soft skills to their roles to create compelling and attractive newsletter, advertisement, digital media, film or web designs based on their clients' needs and goals. Some graphic designers already hold natural creative abilities, while they gain other skills through engaging in on-the-job training, taking online courses and earning industry certifications.Related: Learn About Being a Graphic Designer

Examples of graphic designer skills

Graphic designers use both hard and soft skills to complete their job duties successfully. Examples of common graphic designer skills include:

Creativity and innovation

Graphic designers must brainstorm unique ideas for their clients that make their promotional materials stand out from the competition. They'll use their creativity and innovation abilities to conceptualize ideas and designs that meet client's preferences and requirements. Their designs should look visually appealing, while conveying the client's desired message and speaking directly to the target audience.Related: Creativity Skills: Definition, Tips and Examples

Time management and organization

Whether they work for an organization, design agency or independently, graphic designers are typically in charge of several tasks and accounts at once. They must use their time management and organization skills to properly prioritize tasks and track upcoming deadlines. Some graphic designers may have several projects due in one day, so strong multitasking skills can also help them deliver all of their assignments on time.Related: 10 Ways To Improve Your Time-Management Skills

Computer and software knowledge

Graphic designers spend most of their time on the computer using various software programs to create designs, organize tasks or communicate with clients and employees. They must use common software design tools to edit photos, make videos and build unique promotional materials for clients. Basic technical and computer skills will also be beneficial as they schedule tasks, reach out to clients and build design presentations.

Verbal and written communication

When graphic designers aren't creating digital or print materials, they're typically meeting with clients to discuss deadlines, goals and edits. They also use their communication skills to express and pitch design ideas to clients, managers or other employees. Graphic designers may meet with clients in person or speak with them through video calls, email messages or phone calls. They also rely on their listening abilities to show their understanding of the clients' notes, suggestions or ideas.

Coding

Since many graphic designers may build and update web pages, they should hold basic coding abilities. Those who know more advanced coding languages can design well-made, functional websites that will impress clients. Possessing a basic understanding of Java and HTML languages is a great start for entry-level graphic designers.Related: How To Write Code in 6 Steps

Branding and typography

To build strong designs for clients, graphic designers should have basic branding and typography skills. It’s important for them to know how to perform company research and understand the elements of a client's brand, including their colors, fonts, graphics and logos to convey the client's preferred messaging and to reach their target audience. Typography skills are also important to choose and create well-made fonts using the necessary line-height, tracking, spacing and layout techniques.Related: 40 Design Terms You Need To Know

How to improve your graphic designer skills

There are many ways you can enhance your graphic designer skills and abilities. Follow these steps to improve your graphic designer skills:

1. Take software design courses

It's essential for graphic designers to know how to use basic software design tools. You can enroll in courses online or in person. Some software programs allow you to watch instructional videos to understand how they work. If you'd like to learn how to use advanced features for certain platforms, consider watching videos or attending seminars to strengthen your knowledge and skill set. Having extensive knowledge of various software tools and features can make you stand out to employers.

2. Earn additional certifications

A great way to enhance your design skills is to earn relevant certifications that stand out on a resume. There are a wide variety of certifications available to graphic designers that focus on different subjects, like logo designs, color combinations, layouts and compositions. Certifications are a great way to showcase your advanced knowledge and expertise in a certain graphic design specialty. You can research available certifications and their courses online.

3. Pursue side gigs

A great way to improve your skills and earn supplemental cash is through part-time jobs. Many businesses search online for graphic designers to complete short-term projects for their websites or marketing materials. By pursuing smaller freelance gigs online, you can practice your skills and experiment with new design tools you've recently learned. You may be able to apply the skills gained from your freelance jobs to the responsibilities of your full-time role.Related: 30 Side Gigs To Make Some Side Income

4. Learn additional coding languages

You can impress employers with your advanced knowledge of various coding languages. Sign up for online courses or take in-person classes to gain hands-on training in complex coding. Learning new languages or enhancing your existing coding skills allows you to complete more advanced web design tasks for clients, which can lead to raises or promotions in your current role. It can also look impressive to hiring managers reviewing your resume.Related: How To Practice Graphic Design Skills: Tips and Tricks

Graphic designer skills in the workplace

You can apply your graphic designer skills toward crafting unique and engaging designs for your clients and their audiences. Here are tips to help you effectively use your graphic design skills in the workplace:
  • Craft compelling and unique designs according to clients' needs. You must use your listening and communication abilities to understand what goals clients want to achieve with their marketing materials. The designs you create reflect the clients' overall ideas, meet their expectations and grab the attention of their audience.

  • Use different mediums to tell an engaging story. Use your branding and typography knowledge to craft designs for different mediums and outlets, like social media, online advertisements and websites. You should know which layouts and sizes each medium requires to build fitting designs that convey the story of the client's brand.

  • Pitch design ideas to clients. Regularly meet with clients in-person to pitch your ideas for upcoming designs based on their brand guidelines, preferences and objectives. Use your presentation and communication skills to clearly convey your ideas and plans for success. Be open to any notes, suggestions or feedback you may receive from clients.

  • Meet tight deadlines for many different projects. Clients may assign a wide variety of design tasks each day from print advertisements to web pages. You must be able to shift your focus between different work items each day while ensuring you're staying on task with completing them by their deadlines.

How to highlight graphic designer skills

When you’re pursuing a position as a graphic designer, it’s important to highlight your strengths and capabilities throughout the application process. Here’s how you can feature your relevant skills in resumes, cover letters and interviews: 

Graphic designer skills for resume

It’s important for your resume to list the graphic design skills you possess and highlight your expertise. Mention them in several areas throughout the resume, including your work duties, summary and a separate skills section. Review the job description for the role you're applying for to identify any skills listed in the preferences and requirements sections. Locate these skills and ensure you've listed the ones you have in your resume. You can mention these skills in the experience section by explaining how you used these skills in the past to provide value or bring in impressive results for the company. Try to include numbers or percentages when describing these results to clearly demonstrate your success. Your skills section should list each relevant graphic designer skills you possess.

Graphic designer skills for cover letters

In addition to a resume, you can use a cover letter to provide a hiring manager with a more in-depth review of your professional experience, qualifications and skills. Use this document to share an anecdote related to your professional development or describe how previous roles have prepared you for your prospective position as a graphic designer. Besides listing the skills you possess, you can use a cover letter to highlight how you developed and improved them to showcase your strengths and abilities. 

Graphic designer skills for interviews

Your resume and portfolio of work can help you visually showcase your expertise and prove your creative abilities. The interview is the ideal opportunity for you to demonstrate your organizational and time-management skills. Share examples and stories of moments when you delivered projects under tight deadlines and your process for prioritizing tasks. This shows hiring managers that you're disciplined and can follow a schedule with strict deadlines.
